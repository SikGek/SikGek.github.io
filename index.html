About Me

I was born in South Korea and moved to the United States when I was 10 years old, and have wished to pursue EE my entire life.

During my undergraduate studies, I concentrated on mathematics and fundamentals of machine learning and controls, subsidized by courses in embedded systems, analog and digital circuits, object oriented programming, signal processing and robotics.

I was able to participate in multiple internships and gain experiences with admirable startup companies in Korea thanks to being raised up there, as well as the constant practice of Korean.

Now, I was given the opportunity to study AI and robotics at Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology(KAIST) under the guidance of Professor Chang Dong Hee, with intention to contribute to academia and industry.

    Projects

    • Automated semiconductor tray retreival

      During my internship with Lab2Market, I was tasked with programming a Universal Robot's(UR) collaborative robot to replace manual labor of placing and retreiving semiconductor trays. I used deep learning computer vision to figure out the level of displacement to accurately retreive the trays without damage. Only showing early development videos due to the project being owned by the company (as allowed).

    • Wearable Device using ESP32

      In the video, I am showcasing the heartbeat monitor made with an LED and a photodetector using photoplethosmography and OpenCV. Then, using gaussian mixture model (GMM) it detects abnormal heartbets. In addition to this, I implemented a pedometer and gesture detection using accelerometer.

    • Object retreival vehicle using YOLO and Jetson Nano

      Designed an autonomous vehicle that retreives the specified item it reads through the camera using a pair of mechanical arms attached to a servo motor. Needs some additional model compression.

      GitHub

    • Grocery tracker using CNN and Raspberry Pi

      Developed an apparatus meant to be placed on top of a fridge that would track items going in an out of the fridge using sequential images and object classification. Requires calculation pruning to speed up processing.

      GitHub

    • Pinball Machine using Arduino

      Constructed a pinball machine using laser cut wood, arduino, electrical components and motors. Used solenoid motor as the flipper and DC motors as well as multiple sensors as obstacles. Used non-blocking style to execute multiple actions simultaneously.
